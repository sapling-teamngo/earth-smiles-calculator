Implementation of Semicircular Bunds for Sustainable Water Harvesting in Semi-Arid Regions: A Case Study of Al Mafraq, Jordan

Authors: Omar Yaseen, Mariam Najajrah, Hanadi Yosuf

Supervisor: Dr. Amjad Shatnawi

Institution: [Your University/Organization]

Date: December 2024

Abstract

This research proposes the adaptation and implementation of semicircular bunds (earth smiles) for water harvesting in the semi-arid region of Al Mafraq, Jordan. Using design parameters from WOCAT (World Overview of Conservation Approaches and Technologies) methodologies, we developed an optimized system tailored to local conditions of low rainfall (150-200 mm/year), loamy soils, and moderate slopes (3-8%). Based on computational modeling and WOCAT principles from Benin, we expect significant improvements in water retention and agricultural productivity. The projected outcomes include 40-45% reduction in water runoff and 25-30% increase in soil moisture retention. The research provides a theoretical framework for implementing semicircular bunds in similar semi-arid regions worldwide.

1. Introduction

Water scarcity poses significant challenges for agricultural productivity in semi-arid regions like Al Mafraq, Jordan. With annual rainfall between 150-200 mm and increasing climate variability, innovative water harvesting techniques are essential for sustainable agriculture. Semicircular bunds, also known as "earth smiles" or "half-moons," have proven effective in West Africa for capturing runoff and improving soil moisture (WOCAT, 2023).

This research proposes the adaptation of WOCAT methodology for Jordanian conditions, addressing the specific challenges of:

  • Lower annual rainfall compared to West African benchmarks
  • Different soil characteristics and composition
  • Local agricultural practices and crop patterns
  • Socio-economic constraints of small-scale farmers
Research Approach: This study utilizes computational modeling and WOCAT-based calculations to develop optimized design parameters for semicircular bunds in the Al Mafraq region, with projected outcomes based on established hydrological principles.

3.3 Proposed Implementation Protocol

The proposed implementation would follow WOCAT's established protocol with local adaptations:

  1. Site Preparation: Clear vegetation and mark contour lines
  2. Layout: Use compass method to mark semicircles following contours
  3. Excavation: Dig to specified depth, placing soil downhill to form bunds
  4. Enrichment: Add organic matter and compost
  5. Planting: Install drought-resistant species (olives, almonds)
  6. Mulching: Apply local organic mulch for moisture retention

Based on hydrological modeling and WOCAT principles, the adapted semicircular bunds are projected to demonstrate:

  • Runoff reduction: 40-45% compared to untreated plots
  • Soil moisture increase: 25-30% in the root zone

  • Evaporation reduction: 20-25% through mulching effects

5. Conclusion and Recommendations

The proposed adaptation of WOCAT's semicircular bund methodology for Al Mafraq demonstrates the potential transferability of these water harvesting techniques to semi-arid Middle Eastern conditions. Based on our computational modeling and parameter adaptations:

5.1 Expected Success Factors

  • Proper site selection and contour alignment will be critical for success
  • Local adaptation of dimensions based on rainfall and soil conditions should optimize performance
  • Integration with local agricultural practices and crops is expected to enhance adoption
  • Community involvement in planning and implementation would likely improve long-term sustainability

5.2 Recommendations for Implementation

  • Begin with pilot plots of 0.5-1 hectare to validate projected results
  • Establish monitoring systems for soil moisture, plant growth, and water retention
  • Develop regional-specific design parameters for different agro-ecological zones in Jordan
  • Integrate with other soil and water conservation practices for synergistic effects
  • Provide training programs for local agricultural extension services

5.3 Research Limitations and Future Work

This study presents projected outcomes based on computational modeling and WOCAT methodology adaptations. Actual field validation is required to confirm these projections. Future research should include:

  • Field trials to validate the adapted design parameters
  • Long-term monitoring of soil health and water dynamics
  • Socio-economic studies on adoption barriers and incentives
  • Comparative analysis with other water harvesting techniques

5.4 Policy Implications

The promising projections support further investigation of semicircular bunds for inclusion in national soil and water conservation strategies, particularly in water-scarce regions facing climate change challenges.
